Bangle
Design: Rigid, ring-shaped bracelets that are slipped over the hand onto the wrist.
Materials: Commonly made from metal, wood, glass, or plastic.
Styles: Can be simple and sleek or elaborately decorated with gemstones and intricate designs.
Cultural Significance: In South Asian cultures, bangles are traditionally worn by women and have significant cultural and religious meanings.
Fun Fact: In India, it's common for brides to wear a large number of bangles during their wedding ceremonies as a symbol of health, luck, and prosperity.

Chain Bracelet
Design: Composed of interlinked metal loops, creating a flexible and adjustable bracelet.
Variety: Available in various styles such as snake chains, curb chains, and rope chains.
Materials: Typically made from metals like gold, silver, or stainless steel.
Charm Option: Can be personalized with charms, making them unique and meaningful.
Fun Fact: Chain bracelets are versatile and can be worn alone for a minimalist look or layered with other bracelets for a more eclectic style.

Charm Bracelet
Design: A bracelet featuring small decorative pendants or "charms" that dangle from the chain.
Personalization: Charms often represent significant life events, hobbies, or interests, making each bracelet unique to the wearer.
Materials: Commonly made from metals like gold and silver, with charms that may include gemstones, enamel, or engravings.
History: Charm bracelets have been worn for centuries, with early examples found in ancient Egypt.
Fun Fact: Queen Victoria popularized charm bracelets in the 19th century by wearing them with miniature portraits and locks of hair.

Link Bracelet
Design: Consists of interlocking pieces or links, forming a stylish chain bracelet.
Materials: Often made from gold, silver, or other durable metals, with some designs incorporating gemstones or engravings.
Styles: Ranges from delicate and simple chains to bold and chunky statement pieces.
Versatility: Can be worn on its own for a minimalist look or paired with other bracelets for a stacked effect.
Fun Fact: Link bracelets are a popular choice for both casual and formal occasions, thanks to their versatility and timeless appeal.

Tennis Bracelet
Design: A sleek and flexible bracelet set with a continuous line of gemstones, typically diamonds.
Origin: The name "Tennis Bracelet" came from a famous incident involving tennis player Chris Evert, who lost her diamond bracelet during a match in 1987, prompting a game delay.
Materials: Traditionally features diamonds set in gold or platinum, but modern versions may use various gemstones and metals.
Occasions: Perfect for formal events, weddings, or as a timeless accessory for daily wear.
Fun Fact: Tennis bracelets are valued for their simplicity and elegance, making them a staple in jewelry collections worldwide.
